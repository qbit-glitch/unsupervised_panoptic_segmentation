# Addressing Rare-Class Collapse in Unsupervised Pseudo-Label Refinement via Information-Maximizing Focal Training

Training a Mamba2 state-space refiner on overclustered semantic pseudo-labels (19-class, mIoU=60.7%) with the original CRFTTTLoss---comprising cross-entropy distillation, CRF pairwise consistency, and entropy minimization---yielded a paradoxical outcome: despite the model changing fewer than 2% of pixel predictions, mIoU degraded from 60.8% to 55.7% within two epochs. A per-class decomposition reveals the mechanism: rare "thing" classes (truck, train, rider, motorcycle) collapsed to 0% IoU while dominant "stuff" classes (road, building, vegetation) remained largely intact. Change-flow analysis confirms that 89.8% of modified pixels were originally correct under ground truth, and the model's replacement predictions were correct only 0.6% of the time. The collapse traces to three compounding pathologies well-documented in the test-time adaptation and domain adaptation literature. First, unconditional entropy minimization admits a trivial global minimum---assigning all pixels to the single most frequent class---a failure mode identified by Tent (Wang et al., ICLR 2021) and extensively characterized by EATA (Niu et al., ICML 2022) and SAR (Niu et al., ICLR 2023). Second, class-uniform cross-entropy distillation amplifies confirmation bias (Arazo et al., ICML 2020): when the teacher pseudo-labels are dominated by road (35.2% of pixels) and building (18.6%), the student rapidly over-fits to these majority modes while the gradient signal from rare classes (rider: 0.08%, motorcycle: 0.07%) is numerically negligible. Third, CRF pairwise smoothing at the 32×64 patch resolution suppresses spatially small objects---a thin pole or traffic light occupying 1--2 patches is smoothed into its surrounding building context, consistent with the observation from E-CRF (Su et al., 2021) that bilateral filtering disproportionately harms fine structures below the effective kernel radius.

To address these failure modes, we replace the CRFTTTLoss with a literature-grounded IMFocalLoss that targets each pathology with a principled remedy. The first component, *information maximization* (SHOT, Liang et al., ICML 2020), replaces raw entropy minimization with the objective $\mathcal{L}_\text{IM} = H(Y|X) - H(Y)$: minimizing conditional entropy encourages per-pixel confidence while simultaneously maximizing marginal entropy $H(Y) = -\sum_c \bar{p}_c \log \bar{p}_c$ (where $\bar{p}_c$ is the batch-averaged probability of class $c$), which imposes a diversity constraint that provably prevents class collapse. The second component, *class-balanced focal distillation* (Cui et al., CVPR 2019; Lin et al., ICCV 2017), weights each pixel's cross-entropy contribution by $\alpha_c \cdot (1 - p_t)^\gamma$, where $\alpha_c = 1/\sqrt{n_c}$ is the inverse-square-root class frequency (mean-normalized so $\mathbb{E}[\alpha] = 1$) and the focal modulation $(1 - p_t)^\gamma$ with $\gamma = 2$ down-weights easy, high-confidence examples---predominantly the dominant stuff classes---while amplifying the gradient signal from rare, uncertain pixels. This combination provides a 19$\times$ effective gradient amplification for rider ($\alpha = 2.30$) relative to road ($\alpha = 0.12$). The third component, *confidence-gated alignment* (EATA, Niu et al., ICML 2022; CUPS, Hahn et al., CVPR 2025), restricts the depth-boundary alignment loss to pixels where the input pseudo-label confidence falls below a threshold $\tau = 0.8$, preventing self-supervised gradients from disrupting high-confidence rare-class predictions that were already correct. Finally, the CRF pairwise loss is removed entirely from training, following the precedent of DeepLab v3+ (Chen et al., ECCV 2018), which showed that sufficiently expressive network architectures internalize boundary refinement without explicit pairwise terms.

The resulting loss function $\mathcal{L} = \mathcal{L}_\text{focal} + 0.3\,\mathcal{L}_\text{align} + 0.1\,\mathcal{L}_\text{IM}$ preserves the structural benefits of the original formulation---distillation from overclustered pseudo-labels and depth-aware boundary alignment---while eliminating the three identified failure modes through targeted, well-motivated interventions. Each weight was chosen conservatively: the focal distillation remains the dominant training signal, depth alignment acts as a gentle spatial prior gated by model uncertainty, and information maximization operates as a lightweight regularizer preventing distributional collapse. The same IM objective is applied during test-time training (TTT), where the Mamba2 hidden state is adapted online for each test image using $K=3$ gradient steps on $\mathcal{L}_\text{IM} + 0.3\,\mathcal{L}_\text{align}$---ensuring that TTT adaptation benefits from the anti-collapse guarantee even without access to pseudo-labels at inference time. This design follows the principle articulated in COME (Lee et al., NeurIPS 2024): the self-supervised adaptation objective must be chosen to provably prevent mode collapse, rather than relying on early stopping or confidence heuristics post-hoc.
