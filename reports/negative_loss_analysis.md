# On the Negative Total Loss in Self-Supervised Semantic Refinement

## Loss Landscape Analysis of the CSCMRefineNet Objective

The CSCMRefineNet training objective comprises four self-supervised terms: cross-view consistency $\mathcal{L}_\text{consist}$ (UniMatch V2, TPAMI 2025), depth-boundary alignment $\mathcal{L}_\text{align}$ (DepthG, CVPR 2024), feature-prototype compactness $\mathcal{L}_\text{proto}$, and prediction entropy minimization $\mathcal{L}_\text{ent}$. The total loss is given by:

$$\mathcal{L}_\text{total} = \lambda_c \mathcal{L}_\text{consist} + \lambda_a \mathcal{L}_\text{align} + \lambda_p \mathcal{L}_\text{proto} + \lambda_e \mathcal{L}_\text{ent}$$

with $\lambda_c{=}1.0$, $\lambda_a{=}0.5$, $\lambda_p{=}0.3$, $\lambda_e{=}0.1$. Notably, $\mathcal{L}_\text{total}$ assumes negative values during training. This arises from the prototype compactness term, which maximizes the cosine similarity between DINOv2 patch features and their predicted class prototypes: $\mathcal{L}_\text{proto} = -\frac{1}{N}\sum_{i} \sum_{c} p_i^c \cdot \text{cos}(\mathbf{f}_i, \boldsymbol{\mu}_c) \in [-1, 0]$, where $p_i^c$ denotes the softmax probability of pixel $i$ for class $c$, $\mathbf{f}_i$ is the $\ell_2$-normalized DINOv2 feature, and $\boldsymbol{\mu}_c$ is the probability-weighted class prototype. Since this term is inherently non-positive and its magnitude ($\approx$$-0.62$) exceeds the sum of the remaining non-negative terms early in training, the total loss is driven below zero.

This negative loss regime does not indicate pathological optimization. Rather, it reflects the dominance of a contrastive-style objective within a multi-task loss landscape. The prototype term encourages the refinement network to produce predictions that are maximally consistent with the frozen DINOv2 feature space, which serves as an unsupervised semantic prior. As training progresses, the entropy term decreases from $\sim$3.1 to $\sim$1.2 nats (indicating increasingly confident predictions), the alignment term stabilizes (preserving depth-boundary correspondence), and the consistency term activates once prediction confidence exceeds the threshold $\tau{=}0.9$. The monotonic decrease of $\mathcal{L}_\text{total}$ thus reflects the joint optimization of all four objectives, with the increasingly negative prototype similarity indicating stronger alignment between refined semantic predictions and the underlying visual feature manifold.

A potential concern with this loss configuration is the dominance of $\lambda_p \mathcal{L}_\text{proto}$, which may suppress gradient contributions from the other terms. We monitor this through per-epoch panoptic evaluation (PQ, PQ$_\text{stuff}$, PQ$_\text{things}$) and track gradient norms, which remain bounded within $[0.2, 3.0]$ throughout training. If the panoptic quality plateaus while the prototype loss continues to decrease, this would suggest over-optimization of feature compactness at the expense of boundary precision. In such cases, reducing $\lambda_p$ from 0.3 to 0.1 or introducing a prototype loss warmup schedule would restore balance across the competing objectives. This multi-objective monitoring strategy is consistent with recent practices in self-supervised panoptic segmentation (CUPS, CVPR 2025; CAUSE, Pattern Recognition 2024), where auxiliary losses are tuned based on downstream task metrics rather than individual loss magnitudes.
